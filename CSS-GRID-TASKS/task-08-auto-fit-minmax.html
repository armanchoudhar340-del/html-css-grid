Answers

1. What does 'minmax(200px, 1fr)' do?

It means the column can never be smaller than 200px, but it can grow to take up 1 fractional part of the space if there is room.

2. What is the difference between 'auto-fit' and 'auto-fill'?

auto-fit stretches the items to fill the entire row, while auto-fill keeps the columns at their calculated width even if there aren't enough items to fill the row.

3. Why is this useful for responsive design?

Because it creates a "liquid" layout. As the screen gets smaller, columns automatically drop to the next row when they hit the 200px minimum, making it mobile-friendly instantly.
